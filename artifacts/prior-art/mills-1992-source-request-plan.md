# Mills 1992 source-retrieval gate

Date: 2026-07-22

## Outcome

The bounded retrieval tranche did not locate the full text of W. H. Mills, “The covering number C(11,5,3),” *Utilitas Mathematica* 41 (1992), p. 63, or another primary published proof of uniqueness. The official archive confirms the one-page citation but provides neither an article page nor a file. Its public API has no attached media for volume 41. Internet Archive, Crossref, and OpenAlex returned no scan, DOI, abstract, or alternative copy.

This is an exact source blocker, not mathematical evidence against uniqueness. The 20-class direct reduction remains conditional. No direct-20 CNF was solved, no old certificate gained a new exact equivalence audit, and the certified frontier ledger remains 33/47.

## What a qualifying source must establish

The required premise is stronger than the numerical equality `C(11,5,3)=20`. It must establish that every ordinary 20-block cover of the 3-subsets of an 11-point set by 5-subsets is isomorphic, under an arbitrary permutation of the 11 points, to the maintained representative. A construction or a lower-bound proof alone does not make the 20 forced-matching classes exhaustive.

The 1994 van Rees paper does not supply this theorem. It states that the cover “is also unique” and attributes the assertion to “W.H. Mills. Private communication”; it gives no theorem hypotheses, equivalence definition, or proof. Mills’s separate one-page 1992 note may or may not contain the missing argument and must be inspected rather than inferred from its title.

## Decisive retrieval request

Request a scan of the single page from one physical holder. The exact request is:

> Please supply a scan of page 63 from *Utilitas Mathematica*, volume 41 (1992), ISSN 0315-3681: W. H. Mills, “The covering number C(11,5,3).” The complete item is one page. This is needed to verify whether it proves uniqueness of the optimal covering design, not only the numerical covering number.

Verified holdings routes, in practical order:

1. A local-library interlibrary-loan/document-delivery request using the full citation and ISSN.
2. [LIBRIS serial record](https://libris.kb.se/bib/3651280): Umea holds volumes 1–42, Linkoping holds volumes 41–55, and NTNU reports holdings from volume 1 onward.
3. [CiNii serial record](https://ci.nii.ac.jp/ncid/AA0087932X): several Japanese libraries explicitly cover volume 41, including Osaka, the Institute of Statistical Mathematics, Tohoku, Hirosaki, and Hiroshima.
4. [Current Utilitas Mathematica contact](https://utilitasmathematica.com/index.php/Index): ask the editorial office for the missing legacy page. The maintained [Combinatorial Press volume record](https://combinatorialpress.com/um/vol41/) should be included to show the archive gap.

No request was sent during this tranche; that would be an external communication requiring the user’s authorization or library credentials.

## Receipt protocol

On receipt:

1. Preserve the original scan and URL/email provenance; record byte count and SHA-256.
2. Transcribe the entire one-page note and separately identify theorem, hypotheses, proof, and equivalence relation.
3. Check whether “unique” means ordinary `S_11` point relabeling, a more restricted equivalence, or only uniqueness inside a construction family.
4. Audit that its domain includes links inherited from every hypothetical 40-block `C(12,6,4)` cover without extra unproved restrictions.
5. Promote the 20 residual CNFs only if that scope audit passes. Then audit the eight class-isomorphic legacy certificates and solve only the eleven genuinely uncovered classes.

If the note establishes only `C(11,5,3)=20`, the next proof-relevant route is an independently certified exhaustive classification of ordinary 20-block links; generic residual solving still does not establish a theorem.
