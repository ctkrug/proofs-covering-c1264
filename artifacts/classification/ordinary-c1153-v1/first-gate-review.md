# Ordinary C(11,5,3)=20 classification: first-gate review

Date: 2026-07-22

## Audited deliverable

The candidate theorem domain is unrestricted: exactly twenty distinct 5-subsets of an 11-point set,
covering all 165 triples. No degree, pair-multiplicity, forced-matching, or C(12,6,4)-inheritance
condition is present.

The exact normalization and completeness argument passed independent reconstruction. One selected
block is mapped to `{1,2,3,4,5}` under S11. The remaining blocks are partitioned by the first present
orbit of the root stabilizer `S5 x S6`, equivalently intersection size 4, 3, 2, 1, or 0. The five
standalone CNFs collectively represent every isomorphism class.

The maintained cover's known class was independently regenerated by a different algorithm. There are
7,200 root-normalized images; orbit-incidence double counting gives 166,320 full labeled images and
automorphism order 240. The reusable blocker excludes exactly these images. The verifier independently
reconstructed every coverage clause, every exact-20 dynamic-programming equivalence, every blocker,
every branch clause, the cached shared base, and all five standalone CNFs.

Independent audit status: `VALID_FIRST_GATE`. Classification status: **incomplete**; no solver outcome
has yet been promoted to a certificate.

## Next bounded experiment

First generate external proofs for the three easy leaves under a small fixed cap and replay them. In
parallel only at the planning/build layer, refine the two hard leaves by the same complete
first-present-orbit construction for a third block. The intersection-4 stabilizer has order 2,880 and
produces 17 third-block branches; the intersection-3 stabilizer has order 576 and produces 25. Thus
the next hard-tail discriminator is a fully audited 42-leaf split, sampled before any broad proof run.

Route switches:

- Any validated SAT witness is checked against all 165 triples and the full known orbit; a nonisomorphic
  witness disproves uniqueness and becomes a new blocked class.
- Replay-verified UNSAT on all five current leaves proves ordinary uniqueness directly.
- Severe proof growth or a low closure rate in the 42-leaf sample stops scaling and triggers a deeper
  structural/canonical split, not a blind timeout increase.
- The twenty residual C(12,6,4) CNFs remain untouched and conditional until this classification gate
  completes.
