# C(12,6,4) phase-0 control and discriminator log

All computation in this log ran on the local Mac. It did not consume the droplet core assigned to
the active R(5,5) census. These are baseline and encoding measurements, not an exact-value result.

## Controls

- The frozen published 41-block C(12,6,4) cover passes an independent direct check of all 495
  four-subsets.
- A C(10,4,2) cover with 9 blocks was found in 1.58 seconds after sound symmetry and degree
  constraints and passes the independent direct checker.
- The safe C(10,4,2) at-most-8 sequential-counter CNF, with one fixed block under S10, solved UNSAT
  in 258.79 seconds. Its 375,026,931-byte DRAT proof passed a distinct drat-trim replay under a
  separately built checker. The CNF has only one auxiliary counter family above the 210 primary
  variables, so there is no possible cross-family identifier collision.
- A C(11,5,3) cover with 20 blocks was found in 187.78 seconds after encoding the source-justified
  degree pattern 10,9,...,9. Its 20 blocks pass the independent checker over all 165 triples.

## Safety incident caught during the pilot

An early degree-strengthened C(10,4,2) CNF reused auxiliary identifiers between independently
generated counters. drat-trim correctly verified the emitted but semantically wrong CNF. The result
was immediately invalidated, preserved with `ENCODING-INCIDENT.json`, and assigned no mathematical
meaning. The generator now allocates above the current CNF maximum and records every disjoint
auxiliary range. Positive controls were rerun after the correction.

This incident establishes a permanent design rule: proof replay checks solver-to-CNF correctness;
it does not replace an independent source-to-CNF semantic audit.

## Measured direct-cube tranche

Both root CNFs were partitioned into complete depth-7 frontiers of 128 cubes. An independent checker
rebuilt all Boolean assignments and verified exact, duplicate-free coverage. The frontier hashes are
`2a23aa4d...a3ca` for r=0 and `e8141857...8483c` for no-r=0/r=1.

A deterministic stratified sample tested 64 cubes from each frontier at a 10-second cap. Independent
sample receipts matched every row back to its frozen frontier. Only 10 of 128 cubes returned a
provisional closed verdict; 118 timed out, for a 7.8125% closure rate over 1,253.43 solver-seconds.
No closed leaf is promoted to a proof because this pilot did not preserve and replay a leaf proof.

This is below even the declared 20% yellow threshold. Shallow direct cubing is therefore stopped as
the scale-up route. It remains a useful final completeness fallback only after a materially stronger
branching scheme or encoding.

## Link-first redesign

The first verified C(11,5,3) point link generated a 72,750-variable, 144,806-clause residual CNF.
PySAT's proof stream failed independent replay and was explicitly invalidated. External CaDiCaL then
solved the same CNF UNSAT; its 23,780,099-byte proof, SHA-256
`a253ae84...e97ec8acb`, passed drat-trim. A fresh checker directly validated the link and rebuilt all
230 residual coverage clauses, 55 exact pair constraints, auxiliary ranges, and emitted CNF clauses.
This excludes only that link orbit from extending to a 40-block cover.

### Exact link symmetry

Fixing the degree-10 point and its matched partner leaves the exact group C2 wr S5 of order 3,840.
The verified control link has orbit size 320 and stabilizer 12. A separately written auditor rebuilt
all group actions, the canonical representative, and the exact 320-clause orbit blocker.

An important unsafe shortcut was rejected: the old C(11,5,3) control fixed an arbitrary first block
under full S11, but that unit is not complete after the matching is fixed. Instead, all 462 possible
link blocks were partitioned into six exact block orbits of sizes 80, 120, 10, 32, 160, and 60. The
six disjoint root cases select the least occupied orbit, force earlier invariant orbits empty, and fix
one canonical block within the selected orbit. An independent block-type checker verified that this
partition covers every primary variable exactly once.

The rooted discriminator found a second valid link orbit in root 2 in 13.58 seconds. Its orbit has 16
images and stabilizer 240. Continued canonical augmentation found two more distinct link orbits in
root 1, of sizes 960 and 320. Every witness passed the direct link checker, full-group
canonicalization, and a separately implemented orbit audit. The current four-orbit catalog blocks
1,616 images; its audit-bound catalog SHA-256 is
`cf67941262fde9726f16c850e44082fba6d44685b776ab29c7dcb68905a2e242`.

A second labeled root-1 SAT witness initially appeared to be a fifth orbit, but full-group
canonicalization correctly identified it as another representative of the fourth orbit. It was
recorded as a duplicate, not added to the catalog. This is a permanent rule: stabilizer-root cases
partition labeled assignments but do not by themselves classify full-group link orbits.

After blocking both orbits, root 2 solved UNSAT in 2.75 seconds. Roots 3, 4, and 5 also solved UNSAT
in 0.9-1.9 seconds. Each exact CNF passed clause-by-clause reconstruction, each was rerun with the
pinned external CaDiCaL binary, and all four external DRAT proofs passed the independently built
drat-trim checker.

Roots 0 and 1 were then decomposed under their primary-block stabilizers into 39 and 68 complete
secondary cases. Independent action-based auditors verified both partitions. Root 0 has 32
replay-validated secondary exclusions and seven timeouts; root 1 has 60 validated exclusions and
eight timeouts. Both closure rates exceed 80%, so canonical augmentation passes the continuation
gate even though the monolithic roots timed out.

Root-0/secondary-0 received a third layer under its exact eight-element two-block stabilizer. The
122-leaf partition independently covers all 460 eligible remaining blocks. Eighty-nine leaves now
have reconstructed CNFs and replayed external proofs; 33 remain UNKNOWN. Across the current link
campaign there are 189 hash-bound external proof receipts and an open frontier of 14 secondary cases
plus those 33 tertiary leaves.

The second link's residual CNF has 72,762 variables and 144,830 clauses. External CaDiCaL solved it
UNSAT in 12.17 seconds; its 22,733,396-byte proof, SHA-256
`c122f3f2...b9e1cfed`, passed drat-trim. Its source constraints and every emitted clause were also
rebuilt by the residual auditor. The third and fourth link representatives also produced reconstructed
residual CNFs and replay-verified external UNSAT proofs. Thus all four currently cataloged link orbits
are excluded from extending to a 40-block cover.

## Current decision

The link-first route is promoted over direct shallow cubing. The current immutable campaign summary,
SHA-256 `1a9ff4abe35470308876496219ec6cf93d64e6b10ceafb942a3dd43b5dd3139f`,
records four cataloged orbits, 189 validated external proof receipts, and 47 open frontier nodes. The
next tranche adds one canonical layer only where needed. Every new link orbit is immediately tested
through the residual problem; a SAT residual switches to direct dual validation of a 40-block cover.
UNSAT leaves require CNF reconstruction and external proof replay before they count as exclusions.

No exact value is claimed. The current cardinality reconstruction uses a separate checker but the
same pinned PySAT sequential-counter implementation; a publication-grade global exclusion must add a
second encoding or independently verified cardinality translation. UNKNOWN remains inconclusive.
